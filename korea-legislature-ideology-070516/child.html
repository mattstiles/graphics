South Korean National Assembly Ideology, 2000-2016

The average scores for the two major political parties — Minjoo (more liberal) and Saenuri (more conservative) — on a 15-question ideological test taken at the start of recent sessions of the national legislature. A random sample of South Koreans also answered the questions, allowing a comparison with their (mostly) elected representatives.

Notes: The scores of two smaller but still important (and generally liberal) political groups, the People’s and Justice parties, are not shown because historical data wasn’t released.

Data: Korea JoongAng Daily
